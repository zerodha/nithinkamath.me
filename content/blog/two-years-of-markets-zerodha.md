Two years ago today, we started a daily finance and economics podcast called The Daily Brief.

The initial impulse was me telling the team that there wasn’t really a good Indian podcast on markets, finance, and economics that I wanted to listen to while travelling.

The result was an experiment called The Daily Brief, launched as part of a larger initiative called Markets by Zerodha (very original name, I know).

Fast forward two years, and the [YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/@marketsbyzerodha) has crossed 2.3 lakh subscribers, generated more than 1.6 crore views, and [the newsletter](https://substack.com/@zerodhaonline) has 1.3 lakh subscribers. The Daily Brief has become a daily newspaper of sorts for a wide range of people, from investors, analysts, CXOs, policymakers, students, and anyone curious about what’s happening in the world of business, markets, and economics.

I’m still surprised that people read a ~5000-word newsletter every day as opposed to swiping reels.

The team that runs it is barely 7 odd people, and they’ve gone on to build a whole ecosystem of shows and newsletters, including [Aftermarket Report](https://aftermarketreport.zerodha.com/?utm_source=global-search), [Chatter](https://thechatter.zerodha.com/?utm_source=global-search), [Subtext](https://subtextbyzerodha.substack.com/?utm_source=global-search), [Points and Figures](https://pointsandfigures.substack.com/?utm_campaign=profile_chips), and others, each with an audience and identity of its own.

This goes to show that there’s still a genuine hunger for quality finance content in India. Despite the explosion of content over the last few years, I don’t think that problem is fully solved yet.
