1 week ago, [GalaxEye's](https://x.com/GalaxEye) Mission Drishti reached orbit, and we're proud to have played a small part in their journey through Rainmatter.

When we first met them a few years ago, we were floored by their audacious goal. They were building the world’s first commercial OptoSAR satellite— it would combine optical imaging and radar sensors into a single payload that could see through clouds and operate day or night, no matter the weather conditions. It had never been done before.

Their journey from [IIT Madras](https://x.com/iitmadras) to the [SpaceX](https://x.com/SpaceX) launch on 3 May hasn't been easy. It took 4 years and countless obstacles along the way. It is rocket science after all! 😆 And building something as groundbreaking as Mission Drishti requires a team of founders with incredible grit and belief. This short clip captures the dream, the nerves, and the launch.

Huge congratulations to [Suyash Singh](https://x.com/thesuyashsingh) and the entire team at GalaxEye. We need more Indian startups to build for the world like this. Happy to have backed them in a small way through [Rainmatter](https://x.com/Rainmatterin).
